Lesson 5 — Data, Information & The Digital World

Data is raw facts. Information is data organized into meaning. Computers transform data into insights that shape how we use technology every day.

Activity 1: Data Detective

Imagine we survey the class: favorite apps, foods, or hobbies. Collecting these responses is **data**. Organizing them into charts or graphs turns that into **information**.

Quiz: Which of these is data, not information?


Activity 2: Binary Challenge

Computers store everything in binary: 0s and 1s. For example, the letter “A” is 01000001 in binary.

Which of these is binary?



Reflection Prompts

  • What kind of data do you create every day without realizing it?
  • How do apps use data to make your experience personal?

Wrap-Up & Transition

Data is the fuel of computing. From social media to health trackers, understanding data helps us see how technology works.
